# Learner Profile

## Who They Are
Aspen is a product designer. They came to this hackathon primarily to learn vibecoding — how to use AI to take an idea from concept to working product. Their current project idea is a Pomodoro productivity timer with user-chosen music, auditory cues for each stage, and start/pause/reset controls.

## Technical Experience
**Level:** Beginner. Learns in bursts — picks up just enough to ship an idea every few years, then steps away and forgets. No sustained coding practice.

**Approach:** Heavily visual — needs to see working outputs to know if the code is doing what it should. Prefers tangible, rendered results over reading terminal output or logs.

**Languages/Frameworks:** None established.

**What they want to explore:** Using AI throughout the design and engineering phases of product development.

**AI coding agent experience:** Claude Code is their first AI coding tool. No prior experience with Cursor, Replit, Lovable, v0, or similar.

## Learning Goals
Wants to develop a clearer sense of *when* and *how* to bring AI into the product development process — both in the design phase (ideation, flows, mockups) and the engineering phase (building, iterating). The goal is a repeatable mental model, not just a one-time build.

## Creative Sensibility
Strong fantasy UI aesthetic preferences. Referenced:
- **Baldur's Gate 3** (ornate, high-fantasy, detailed UI)
- **Disco Elysium** (painterly, literary, atmospheric)
- **Elder Scrolls Online** (classic fantasy MMO UI)

Signals: parchment textures, decorative borders, jewel tones, serif/gothic typography, immersive and thematic UI over minimalism. Uses Discord and Bluesky socially.

**Design direction:** Lean into fantasy game UI aesthetics for the Pomodoro app — this is a meaningful differentiator from typical productivity tool design.

## Prior SDD Experience
Yes — significant, though framed as design practice rather than development process. Aspen regularly creates user flows before building and produces multiple rounds of mockups for developer handoff. They understand the value of structured pre-work; this curriculum extends that instinct into the engineering phase.

**Calibration for /reflect:** Aspen already practices SDD on the design side. Quiz and reflection should focus on how the engineering-side planning (PRD, spec, checklist) compares to and connects with their existing design process, and what was new or surprising.
